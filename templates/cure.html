Fungal Infection - Cure

1)Ringworm: Prevent it by keeping your body clean and dry. It’s easily spread, so don’t share towels, combs, or other personal items.

2)Athlete’s Foot: Prevent it by wearing shower shoes at the gym, washing your feet daily, drying them well, and wearing clean socks.

3)Nail Fungus: Prevent nail fungus by keeping hands and feet clean and dry, wearing dry socks and changing them often, wearing shoes in a public shower, pool, or locker room, and not scratching infected skin, such as athlete’s foot.

4)Other: Change out of your gym clothes right after a workout. Sweaty gear provides a perfect home for fungi and other germs to thrive and grow. Wash exercise clothes after each use.

Allergy - Cure

1)Shut Out Breezes: It’s a gorgeous day. But if the pollen count is high, keep the windows and doors closed to protect your indoor air.

2)Wash Up: After being outdoors, your clothes, shoes, hair, and skin are covered with tiny particles from everywhere you’ve been. Take a shower and change your clothes to wash away any allergens.

3)Wear a Mask: Were respirator mask, it will block 95% of small particles, such as pollen and other allergens.

4)Eat Healthy: there’s no doubt that a healthy diet is good for your whole body. Add at least one fresh fruit and veggie to every meal.

5)Drink More: If you feel stuffy or have postnasal drip from your allergies, sip more water, juice, or other nonalcoholic drinks.

6)Avoid Cigarette Smoke: Choose smoke-free restaurants, nightclubs, and hotel rooms. Avoid other fumes that can make your symptoms worse, too, like aerosol sprays and smoke from wood-burning fireplaces.

GERD - Cure

1)Maintain a healthy weight: Excess pounds put pressure on your abdomen, pushing up your stomach and causing acid to reflux into your esophagus.

2)Stop smoking: Smoking decreases the lower esophageal sphincter's ability to function properly.

3)Don't lie down after a meal: Wait at least three hours after eating before lying down or going to bed.

4)Eat food slowly and chew thoroughly: Put down your fork after every bite and pick it up again once you have chewed and swallowed that bite.

5)Avoid tight-fitting clothing: Clothes that fit tightly around your waist put pressure on your abdomen and the lower esophageal sphincter.
